The Evidence Based Education podcast has the 3rd installment in their series on teacher collaboration posted today. There are 3 different scholars interviewed, but what caught my ear was the middle segment with Jenni Donohoo. The research team published this study about what conditions were present in schools that were able to take well-documentented teaching and learning practices (e.g. Hatties' lists) and put them into widespread practices. Here is the list of the necessary conditions:

  1. The presence of a learning methodology; 
  2. clear examples of how to apply the strategies; 
  3. a ‚Äòknowledgeable other‚Äô to help assist educators in processing the research; 
  4. a supportive organizational environment; 
  5. the recognition of educators as agents of influence, and 
  6. the monitoring and adjustment of implementation strategies.
