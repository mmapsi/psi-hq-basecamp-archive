‚ú® Highlights from CA Assessment Conference (Oct. 2020)

Emily attended the California Assessment Conference on our behalf last month. Below are highlights from the sessions she attended. Please reach out to Emily if you have more questions. Thanks, Emily!

The California Department of Education held a virtual conference in October. Here are some resources from different workshops that could be of interest to you and your clients:

  • Smarter Tools for Teachers (smartertoolsforteachers.org) is an interactive website that educators can use to find sample lessons in ELA or math based on the standard. It's search-friendly. It's published by Smarter Balanced.
  • A Jamboard is a great tool to introduce the Frayer Model in distance learning.
  • The English Learner Toolkit of Strategies contains a plethora of sample activities to use with ELs that are easily adaptable to distance learning. (You have to download it).
  • Flipgrid is a great resource for EL students to practice speaking and for their teachers to assess their oral skills. Plus, instead of written feedback, a recorded response from the teacher can help students practice their listening skills.
  • Rubrics are a great tool, but they can be misguided. Can you define what a rubric is, vs. what a rubric does? Rubrics can be too short, too long or overall too complicated. Student-generated rubrics build agency and independence.The conversations that students have around creating a rubric can also inform the teacher about their students' understanding of the task they are engaging in. Students can use rubrics with their peers to justify self-scoring, or why they gave a particular score to their partner. Confidence regarding self assessment of the assigned task has the biggest impact on student agency (Hattie). 
  • -Did you know that on the SBAC, multiple choice answers with a circle require only one circle to be bubbled in, but that multiple choice questions with a square next to the choices indicate there is more than one answer?
