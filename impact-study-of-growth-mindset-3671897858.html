👍 Impact study of growth mindset

There have been lots of studies that show both strong and weak impacts of growth mindset interventions on student outcomes. The majority of studies show positive results for traditionally underserved students and mixed results for other students. There is a recently published international study that evaluates the impact of a growth mindset intervention across 600,000 students in 78 countries. The study teases apart some of the differential impact - where students do and don't get significant benefits.

What also struck me was this quote from Carol Dweck that lines up so well with Partners TOA:

“Maybe the most important thing we learned was teachers’ mindsets mattered,” Dweck said. “It was when the teachers themselves held more of a growth mindset that they created classroom cultures in which students’ new and improved growth mindset could take root and turn into enhanced achievement.”
