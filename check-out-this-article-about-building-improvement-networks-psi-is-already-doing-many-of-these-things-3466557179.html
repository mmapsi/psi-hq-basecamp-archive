Chris Thorn, Chief Program Officer
I see two classic improvement problems. 
1) It's for sure a trope in improvement science that more effort (or expertise) is not a way out of problems.  PDSA thinking can become a cognitive trap that gets people trapped in a local set of conditions when perhaps there is a radically different way to achieve the same goal - that's innovation/redesign, an often forgotten aspect of improvement work. 
2) It also seems to speak to the more traditional model of school improvement which has been to just keep adding more and more that eventually breaks the camel's back.
