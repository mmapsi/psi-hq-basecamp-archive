Teaching Book Rec - Start Here, Start Now - A guide to antibias and antiracist work in your school community - Liz Klienrock

Hey Everyone,

I just finished reading through this book by Liz Klienrock and thought it was really great for the following reasons:

  • It's got a lot of practical suggestions, most of which are familiar ideas to teachers.
  • Her writing is great with a good balance of anecdotes and how the activities she suggest contribute to a antibias and antiracist classroom culture.
  • She did a lot of work to gather perspectives of parents, students, administrators and other teachers to add to her own perspectives.
  • The book is organized into chapters with common themes people ask about ABAR work like: What does ABAR look like if all or most of my students are white? and What does ABAR look like if I teach STEM subjects?
Sounds awesome!
