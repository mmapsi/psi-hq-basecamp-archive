Resource Needed: School Reopening Processes/Plans

Hello all, 

Two leaders I support have been tasked with creating a "Reopening Plan".  They (and I) don't have much more information than that so our first step is to look out to other districts to understand the processes teams engaged in to develop their plans and if any reopened, how they landed on their final plan.  Any direction you can provide is greatly appreciated.

Jae Fusco, Supervising Improvement Partner
Hey Alex ,

Truthfully, I've only seen bare bones here in Philly because everything has been postponed. I am sharing a very simplistic PPT that was shown to principals in November when they originally thought we would start hybrid instruction for grades K-2. It shows a vague plan of document cameras existing in rooms to support instruction, PD for teachers, and allotted time for teachers to prepare their classrooms:

https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/1USdH1cCeu9POWHcqp7Z1KdL94lGaoO222wqSYz_eM4g/edit?ts=5fa2cbbf#slide=id.ga4a0f95e4d_0_45

The admin team had to complete a building inventory in order to build a plan to pick the best classrooms to have in person instruction and the communicated expectation was to have updated schedules carving out transition time, logistics of movement within the building, and arrival and dismissal procedures. Admin were also asked to ideate about an A/B in person instruction cohort with a schedule. Wish I had more to offer!
