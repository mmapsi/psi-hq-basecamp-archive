APM Reports has been focused on both sharing the scientific consensus about how to teach early reading. More recently, the work has focused on how the two most popular curricula Caulkins work and Fountas and Pinnell continue to push approaches that are unlikely to be successful struggling readers.

Ignoring reading science has been the standard strategy. Now, it seems that states and districts are finally incorporating this research in their standards. The curricula and PD based on bad science are being removed from the list of acceptable materials.

Amanda Bachelor, Director of Program Design and Development
I used the reader's and writer's workshop model and I was NEVER under the impression that I shouldn't teach kids to sound out words. I think the curriculum is taking WAY more heat than what is warranted. We always used a phonics program along with reader's workshop. In reader's workshop you focused on the habits of good readers, the joy of reading, comprehension, character study, themes... In phonics you learned explicit decoding strategies, sight words, etc that you then applied during reader's workshop. In my opinion, debunking a curriculum that is all about the joy of reading is a bad idea. "Rebalance" yes. We should ALWAYS be improving how we teach kids to read.
