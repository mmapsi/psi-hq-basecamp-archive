Sharing this article with you all about the MOVE bombing that occurred on this day, May 13, 1985 in Philadelphia. Philadelphia police attacked the home of Black liberation and environmentalist group MOVE with automatic weapons, then dropped a bomb on it, killing 5 adults and 6 children, destroying 61 homes in the predominantly Black neighborhood, making 250 people homeless. You can read the article to learn more. Very few people know about this and discuss it. To be honest, I learned about this gruesome event last year while planning an interdisciplinary unit on advocacy. A few grade teams planned to focus on Philly history (1775 - Present Day). One topic that we considered exploring was the MOVE bombing to have students think about advocacy across subjects. Teachers were tasked to backwards plan and identify content, standards, and skills to align to the topic. Last month, April 2021, it was revealed that anthropologists at Princeton and UPenn had the bones of one of the children killed in the bombing, unbeknownst to the families. Just sharing with hopes of raising consciousness and inspiring action.
