Sustaining change for the long haul

At the visioning session, one of the directions discussed was placed-based collective impact. This week, the Collective Impact Forum released a new podcast addressing the question, "How Do You Sustain Your Initiative Over the Long Term?" While we might not pursue this specific strategy, the advice about supporting long-term change in the districts we support is completely applicable to our current work. In particular, they ground their advice based on a framework from the Tamarack Institute that lays 7 aspects of The Sustainability Circle. 
  1. Leadership competence - activities that contribute to high-quality initiatives are the responsibility of leaders
  2. Effective collaboration - involves identifying relevant stakeholders who actively support program goals and have clearly identified roles and responsibilities
  3. Understanding the community - entails having knowledge of community needs and resources; having respect for community members, and involving key community members in programs
  4. Demonstrating results - evaluating process and outcomes using acceptable research methods and informing stakeholders of the results of those evaluations is critical
  5. Strategic funding - includes having plans and resources in place to support current and prospective program requirements
  6. Staff involvement and integration - the inclusion of committed, qualified staff in program design, implementation, evaluation, and decision making develops a culture that values broad-based participation in working toward program sustainability and success
  7. Community responsivity - the ability of a CI initiative to adapt programming to meet changes in community needs is critical
