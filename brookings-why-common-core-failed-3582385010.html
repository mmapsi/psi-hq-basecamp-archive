This piece by Tom Loveless summarizes part of his new book on the same topic. The big takeaway is that the standards movement has always been too top-down and too far away from the what (curriculum) and how (instruction) of how student learning is produced in the classroom. It's not just about expectations embedded in the standards. There are lots of organizational layers between the standards and classroom instruction where things can go off the rails. The standards don't do a thing to address the disparities that already exist when students arrive at school for the first time. There's no simple solution in this analysis unless it is the journey we are on - careful experimentation at the classroom and school level to map out what works for the adults and students who are in that place at that time.
