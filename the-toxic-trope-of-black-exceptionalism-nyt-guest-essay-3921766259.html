☀️ The Toxic Trope of Black Exceptionalism: NYT guest essay

This is a powerful guest essay by Samuel Getchew, Oakland Tech graduate and the Oakland Poet Laureate in 2019. The essay was published in the NYTimes today. The full title is: Black Valedictorians & the Toxic Trope of Black Exceptionalism. Getchew argues that Black valedictorians should be the norm, not the exception, but systems are structurally racist and conduct racialized tracking. 

Here's an excerpt:

In his graduation speech last month (published in the Guardian The Atlantic), Mr. Muhammad (valedictorian this year) pointedly asked why it took 106 years for Oakland Tech to award this honor to a Black male student: “So why me?” he asked. “I don’t know. But for all of those who didn’t get to maximize their potential, for all those who had the ability but lacked the opportunity, I owe it to them to appreciate this history made by the people who put me in this position. We owe it to them to make sure that, while I may be the first young Black man to be our school’s valedictorian, I won’t be the last.”
