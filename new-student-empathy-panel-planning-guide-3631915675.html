As Uchenna shared in the Partners Post, the recent Student Empathy Panel at the ESA Middle Grades Impact Team convening was a highly successful strategy that helped elevate student voice and supported educators to bring their emotions and full selves into their work.

To help capture the "how" of facilitating a Student Empathy Panel, we compiled the various resources that ESA used to plan into a new Student Empathy Panel Planning Guide. Thanks team ESA for sharing this highlight of elevating student voice in your network!

Here are some Student Empathy Panel Planning resources: 
  • ESA's Student Empathy Panel Planning Guide (new!) 
  • Tools to empower student voice

Background info:
On Wednesday, March 24, 2021, the ESA Middle Grades Impact Team held a Student Empathy Panel during their network convening.  Uchenna and Amanda   and school leaders on the ESA Middle Grades Impact Team coordinated a panel of 12 students from across their schools. The goal was to give students voice in their classrooms and communities while providing an opportunity for network participants to hear directly from students about topics related to work they are doing. 

The results were powerful. There were many moments when students were vulnerable, sharing their fears about mathematics, the impact of virtual learning on their ability to succeed, and the ways that words and actions of their teachers and peers presented barriers to learning. 

Hearing from students also surfaced emotions and learning for the teachers and leaders in the audience. Teachers got some concrete ideas for how to support their math students better in a virtual setting. The experience highlighted just how difficult it has been for teachers to feel so little connection with their students this year due to virtual learning, and how that loss of human connection has impacted educators.
