Racial Disparities in Teacher Eval

A new study of the Chicago Public Schools teacher evaluation system results was published recently. The study shows that teachers of color are more likely to be assigned to under-resourced schools. The analysis suggests that it is the local context/school and classroom assignment that explains much of the differences between the evaluation scores of white teachers and teachers of color.  Here's an interview with one of the study authors on CPRE KnowledgeHub and here's a link to the Marshall Memo's synopsis of the study.
