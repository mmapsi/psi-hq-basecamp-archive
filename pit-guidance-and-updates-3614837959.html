Happy Monday!

In our last Dave Dive we calibrated around 5.2.3 (a coaching item in the cycle phase). After reviewing the notes, I wanted to summarize the common ground as guidance for your next cycle review.

Tips to remember when completing item 5.2.3 for this year:
  • Coaching relationships and cycles may look somewhat different this year for a variety of reasons (being responsive to coachee needs, limited access to coachees, inability to meet one to one, unable to observe, using short term goals and adjusting as we go).
  • Coaching frequency, objectives and style should look different in different models.
Success for 5.2.3  includes:
  • Assess progress to date on goals (goals might not have metrics attached or be annual goals, but there is something that the coachee is intentionally working on, ideally aligned to a framework such as CAF, STR, a district framework).
  • Results in an adjustment or shift of some sort on the coachee's part (maybe even shifting to a new goal?)
  • Includes reflection on ways of working together
  • Coachee must be bought into the goal and the coaching relationship for this to be full success.
Success for some might also involve observing the coachee, having both short and long term goals that are measurable and ensuring that reflection happens in a single one to one coaching conversation. But these pieces are not required.

Here's what else you should know to stay on top of your PIT:
  • In our next data dive, we'll be looking at trends in PIT data as we also make connections to the Close & Renew phase. Agenda coming soon!
  • Please make sure your PIT is up to date! After April, we will no longer be marking off items from the Parter, Set Goals, and Plan phases.
  • Watch out for a Tableau Dashboard update coming soon! We will be replacing the full line item description with a shortened version. Full line item description will still appear in some of the pop ups when you hover.
