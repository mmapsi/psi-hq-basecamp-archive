Reflection for Today

Ta-Nehisi Coates on the last four years and the history of white supremacy in America that allowed it to happen. Today marks a long awaited transition, but it doesn't erase what is thought, felt, lived out, and spoken by those who embody (consciously) a belief (or passively, are privileged unconsciously) that white people (particularly white men) achieve with minimal qualification (while others must achieve with maximal effort).

For Coates, he reflects on the original purpose of knighthood - "to serve as defenders of the Faith, upholders of justice, champions of the oppressed." How do we compare that to the reality - namely, how people of color view and are inequitably impacted by police officers, public officials, and others with an ability to assume power simply because they are white? “When the gap between ideal and real becomes too wide,” Tuchman writes, “the system breaks down.”

I hope this read allows for some deep reflection, as it has for me.
