Leadership in Crisis

There have been several things coming across my feeds in the last week that focus explicitly on leading during a crisis. The latest CPRE Research Minutes' most recent podcast "Educational Leadership in Challenging Times" was an interview of the authors of a recent guide for leaders - Jonathan Supovitz and John D'Auria. They position Improvement Science inside a framework of relationships and caring that leaders need to display. In particular, they focus on how leading in a crisis is different from normal times.

I've also taken a close look at a blog post from Dave Snowden of Cognitive Edge that compares the competencies for leaders in the Framework for Leadership in Complexity and competencies for systems in Managing complexity (and chaos) in times of crisis. I really appreciated his comparison of the more simplistic notion of the leadership competencies located only in the leader and the notion of the need for leadership competencies to be distributed - particularly as complexity increases.
