Resource for Restarting Schools

A colleague from Turn Around for Children shared this brief – Reimagine & Rebuild: Restarting School with Equity at the Center – created by a collaboration of education research, advocacy and practice organizations. It highlights a set of restorative practices that are fully aligned with a Whole Child purpose, including centering relationships among students, families, and educators; addressing the comprehensive needs of children; strengthening staffing and partnerships; making teaching and learning rigorous and relevant; and empowering teams at the school and district levels to reimagine and rebuild systems.

Passing this along as you are working with schools to Close and Renew and look ahead to next year.

Chris Thorn, Chief Program Officer
There's a lot of research on information management in crises. I think you are on target Alex. It's always true that less is more. The first move is usually to provide one or two options, that might not be the most obvious next moves. The challenge is to get alternatives into the set of a set of possible choices leaders are considering.

One way people talk about this is "shifting the Overton Window". Older research points to how advisors can shift the "policy choice set" of leaders. I think this aligns pretty well with the change management thinking in the Partners Approach. I big part of off adult mindset work is shifting notions of agency and what is possible. This is true for actors at all levels of the system. 

So, yes, unfiltered resources will undoubtedly be overwhelming. The question is what two or three things might be important to shift a leader's thinking into a set of choices that are more likely to be productive?
