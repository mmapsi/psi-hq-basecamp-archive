We know you are busy with STRs, but as we near the close and renew phase, we wanted to highlight his "19/20 Greatest Hit" from Amanda .

She shared a process for how she did a purposeful Close & Renew with staff at King Elementary School in a time of challenging staff morale at the end of last school year in West Contra Costa. Check it out for ideas & inspiration as you begin planning to support your schools in this process for this school year...
 
Process: Over the past few years, Amanda & her school have gotten good at looking back to plan forward. In 2018, they used a Journey Map as part of Close & Renew to connect with positive things when the morale was low/after a challenging year.
 
Through the Close & Renew process, they generated a culture of shared ownership (vs â€œprincipal fix itâ€ mentality) among the staff and teachers. Here are some key steps she took to make that happen: 
  • Used â€œobstacles design thinkingâ€ process with Close & Renew
  • Enrolled key stakeholders - e.g. teachers are part of design, ILT team, resistant teachers - get their feedback in advance
  • Enforced attendance at the meeting - built the close and renew meeting into the PD schedule
  • Kept the work alive all year - the journey map and obstacles design posters lived - came with them to all the meetings the following year, and the staff used them to inform future decisions & planning. This showed that they were not going away & the value of investing time in the Close & Renew process.

Results:
  • They have fine tuned the PLP each year using the Close & Renew process, and because of this it has become a key document accessible to all staff. 
  • This distributed ownership culture has led to increased teacher retention
  • In addition, this kind of thinking has helped clarify roles of different teams (e.g. ILT, positive behavior support team, system teams)
