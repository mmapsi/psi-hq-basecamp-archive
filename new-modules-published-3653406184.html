New Modules Published

Hi all,

I hope everyone is having a great Monday.  Wanted to share that we have a batch of new modules (slide deck, agenda, and materials) that just got published. These are all the result of a major team effort- folks developing new content for their networks and our internal learning systems, module reviewers, PLC teams working on new RCCP content, PDS team members, etc.  Special highlight to the student voice module which is based off of some amazing work in Philadelphia and ESA to host student panels as part of their networks! See links and descriptions below and you can find the entire catalogue of modules available here and in Google Drive.

Student Voice: Student Panel, Training on Empathy Interviews- This module provides an opportunity to explore ways to empower student voice in equity-focused improvement work. Participants will have a shared experience of student voice through a student Personal Experience Panel (PEP) and then will have time to explore a variety of strategies for continued work around student voice at their site.

Discourse 1 & 2- This module provides an introduction to the importance of the type of discourse we engage in as we address substantive issues in our schools.

White Supremacy Culture and White Body Supremacy- This module provides an opportunity for participants to unpack white supremacy culture and white body supremacy over the course of a 90 minute session. 

Internalized Oppression- This module is designed to support groups to gain an understanding of internalized oppression. It is designed for small to large groups capable of continuous RCCP work.

Unit planning/backwards mapping protocol for math teachers- This module provides a way for teachers to prepare for an upcoming unit. It is designed as a repeat process, leveraging a compiled set of middle grades resources, that can be used at the beginning of each unit over the course of a year. The protocol ensures that teachers are spending time deeply unpacking their upcoming standards to ensure that their instruction is rigorous and standards-aligned.  

Would love to hear from anyone who tests these out and keep the requests for content coming!
