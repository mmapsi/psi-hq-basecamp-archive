There are more and more news and scholarly outlets sending clear signals that "making up for lost learning" is something there is already solid research on. It's also clear that in all but the most challenging situations, acceleration that focuses on assessed, specific learning needs is more effective than reteaching a boatload of missed content.  One body of research focuses on Students with Interrupted Formal Education (SIFE). This CPRE Research Minute discusses SIFE research recommendations. The conservative Fordham Institute also has a podcast on the same topic that discusses the research on acceleration and SEL supports with David Steiner, executive director of the Johns Hopkins Institute for Education Policy.
